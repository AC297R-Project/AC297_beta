Random Portfolio

We use our previous year data as our training period and find the according optimized market. We then use this market to calculate betas for the current year.

For example, we create a random portfolio for 2006. Through simulated annealing, we find the best market for that year. This market is then used to hedge against the S&P using the 60-day rolling betas calculated against the S&P.

We train on 2006, test on 2007; train on 2007, test on 2008; and train on 2008, test on 2009. This is to see how portfolios behave in different market conditions: calm years, volatile years and sharp crashes followed by recoveries.
