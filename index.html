Beta hedging is a method of reducing systemic (market) risk. This is usually accomplished by going short some broad market index fund, such as the S&P 500 tracking index SPY. We used stochastic optimization to find a different hedging market to effectively reduce volatility and correlation with the S&P 500. We found that beta hedging reduces overall returns, but prevents sharp drops in value during market crashes.  We were generally able to match the performance of traditional S&P beta hedging, but not outperform it.
