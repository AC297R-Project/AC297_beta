Beta Hedging

Beta: A measure of the exposure of a portfolio to systemic risk.
High magnitude beta = market moves, portfolio moves along with it.
Beta close to zero = portfolio movements are uncorrelated with market movements.






 Standard Beta Hedging:
1. Find the beta value as the ordinary least squares regression coefficient over the previous 60 days (“rolling OLS”).
2. Go short beta shares of S&P 500 (use an index fund to do this).
Same as going long -beta shares.
3. Enjoy reduced exposure to systemic risk.
4. Hopefully still have some returns left over.

Our Beta Hedging:
1. Find beta each day.
2. Go short beta shares of a different market, composed of a small subset of the securities in the total stock market.
3. Still enjoy decreased exposure to systemic risk.
4. Have lower volatility of returns than with standard beta hedging.


Simulated Annealing

We use simulated annealing to find the market that best reduces correlation of a given portfolio with the S&P 500 over some training period, then use that market as the hedging market.

Our simulation begins with a given portfolio, as well as a random market.

Hedge Class

We downloaded complete stock market data from 2002-2015 (about 4000 stocks) and implemented a class that automatically cleans data and selects valid stocks in a given date range, constructs random portfolios and markets, updates markets during simulated annealing, calculates daily beta forecasts, performs hedging, and calculates returns. This can be found in Hedge.py.
